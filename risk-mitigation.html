Risk Managment and Mitigation

<<Back to main page

To carry out the project risk assessment and formulate our risk register, our team followed the four step risk management process which involves the identification, analysis, planning, and monitoring of potential risks.


Identification

The risk management process began with risk identification where two of our team members worked to create a list of possible risks to the project. The list of risks was then categorised into groups by type. For example, the Estimation risks discussed potential issues relating to the scheduling of our project. This grouping helped us to identify gaps and overlaps in our initial list of risks as well as spot risks that were unnecessary to consider.


Analysis

The risks were then analysed one by one. This was done by rating each riskâ€™s likelihood and severity on the scale of low, moderate, and high. We decided not to use a more fine-grained classification system because it felt unnecessary for the risks we devised.


Planning

Following that, the team proceeded to the planning stage where we agreed on protocols for each risk in order to mitigate its effects. Each risk was also assigned an owner to monitor occurrences of that risk and to ensure the correct protocol is followed to handle it. In order to increase the bus factor for our risk mitigation strategy, it was decided that secondary owners would be allocated to each risk. Furthermore, a single type of risk had multiple team members allocated to it with the purpose of ensuring that each risk type still received attention if a member of the team became unavailable.


Monitoring

Throughout the project, the owners monitored their corresponding risks, keeping note of the statuses of each risk and recording the dates of risk occurrences. This was accomplished as the beginning of our weekly Thursday group meetings was allocated to reading through the risk register and discussing the status of each risk.


Risk Register

Our risk register is organised in a spreadsheet where each row represents a project risk. Each risk is specified by its own ID and a description of what the risk is on the project and is categorised by its risk type. The risk register contains ratings given for the likelihood and severity of each risk as well as the mitigation strategy to be followed in the event of the risk occurring.
